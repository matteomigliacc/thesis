\section{Introduction}

\subsection{Background, Problem, and Analytical Framework}

On 26 November 2025, Regulation 2025/2518 laying down additional procedural rules on the enforcement of Regulation (EU) 2016/679\autocite{ProceduralRegulation} (hereinafter `Procedural Reglation') was adopted. 
The Procedural Regulation seeks to further proceduralize the consistency and cooperation mechanism of the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)\autocite{GDPR} in cases involving cross-border enforcement.

The GDPR is the cornerstone of the European Union's (EU) so-called `digital acquis'.\autocite[223]{zanfirGDPREDPS} 
Entered into force in 2018, and replacing the Data Protection Directive of 1995, it has often been heralded as the most comprehensive package of data protection and privacy regulations.\autocite[498]{mustertProceduralisingEnforcement2026} 
The GDPR operationalizes the fundamental right to data protection, as enshrined in primary EU Law.\autocites[8]{CFR}[16]{TFEU}

The GDPR seeks to provide a harmonized and directly applicable legal framework for the processing of personal data across the EU, increasing legal certainty and strenghtening enforcement.\autocite[recital 7]{GDPR}

However, the GDPR's enforcement mechanism has encountered practical challenges, particularly in the context of cross-border enforcement.

\subsubsection{The Enforcement Challenges of the GDPR}

The GDPR relies on a system of decentralized enforcement.\autocite[recital 1]{ProceduralRegulation} 
Member States (MS) are to establish independent Data Protection Authorities (DPAs), who are competent to carry out enforcement in their national territory.\autocite[51(1) and (2)]{GDPR}

Cross-border enforcement is handled through a One-Stop-Shop (OSS) system,\autocite[recital 127]{GDPR} through the cooperation and consistency mechanism.\autocite[chapter VII]{GDPR} 
When an alleged infringement concerns processing taking place in more than one MS, the DPA of the MS where the main establishment of the controller or processor is located assumes the role of Lead Supervisory Authority (LSA).\autocite[56(1)]{GDPR}
The LSA acts as the sole interlocutor of the accused.\autocite[63]{facebookBelgium}
Other DPAs with a vested interest in the case become Supervisory Authorities Concerned (SAC), and are to cooperate with the LSA in investigating the infringement.\autocite[60]{GDPR}
In case cooperation fails, the consistency mechanism is triggered,  involving the European Data Protection Board (EDPB) as a settler of disputes between the LSA and the SACs.\autocite[arts 63-66]{GDPR}

Multiple scholars and stakeholders of data protection law have noted that the GDPR's enforcement design has suffered from numerous flaws.
Institutional actors and scholars have signaled a lack of true cooperation between DPAs, due to differences in administrative procedure,\autocites[324-325]{mustertCrossborderEnforcementGDPR}[1, 3, 4]{EDPBLetterEUComm} diverging national priorities,\autocite[806]{gentileDeficientDesign2022} and weak procedural rights for complainants in cross-border cases.\autocite[800]{gentileDeficientDesign2022} 

Another concern rests on the role the LSA plays in the cooperation procedure. 
The cooperation mechanism requires the Supervisory Authorities (SAs) to cooperate through information exchange and mutual assistance in with the aim of reaching consensus.\autocites[60(1) and (2)]{GDPR}[point 111]{fbBelgiumAgBobek}
In practice, the LSA has been enabled to exert significant influence over the scope of an investigation, the determination of corrective measures, and the enforcement of those measures.\autocite[809]{gentileDeficientDesign2022}

\subsection{Research Gap}
The Procedural Regulation will enter into force on 2 April 2027. 
Mustert and Hofmann provided early commentary on the Commission Proposal in 2024 while it was in the trilogue stage,\autocite{hofmannFutureGDPREnforcement2024} and Mustert provided an early comment on the Regulation after its passing.\autocite{mustertProceduralisingEnforcement2026} 
However, no structured assessments of the reformed cooperation procedure against EU primary law have been conducted. 
This thesis will attempt to conduct such an assessment, following Mustert's example,\autocite{mustertCrossborderEnforcementGDPR} albeit at smaller scale.

As seen in early commentary, the Procedural Regulation does not seek to harmonize national administrative procedure, but instead acts to require consensus at an earlier stage of the cooperation procedure, in the hopes that enforcement will be more consistent and participated.
